      Vehicle Dispatching – Introduction

      Dispatching of vehicles for pull-in and subsequent service – use automatic suggestions or manually set stabling positions; focus on shunting-free pull-out conditions.

      Purpose and Classification

      The task of vehicle dispatching is to assign a stabling position in the depot to each vehicle and to ensure smooth operations.

      • Ensuring planned blocks and monitoring current vehicle movements.
      • Selection of suitable stabling positions so that a pull-out is possible without shunting and without moving other vehicles.
      • Automatic dispatching by the system; decisions can be manually changed.
      • Demarcation from block dispatching: Vehicle dispatching = physical assignment vehicle↔stabling position; block dispatching = logical assignment block↔vehicle.

      Considered Factors

      Vehicle dispatching considers the following factors when assigning stabling positions:

      • Vehicle characteristics: Vehicle type, range/SoC, fault level, line restrictions
      • Planned reservations of the vehicle to be dispatched (workshop, supply, washing orders)
      • Stabling position characteristics: Track lengths, usage types, type admissibility, charging points
      • Characteristics of the planned blocks
      • Topological dependencies at the depot
      • Configured dispatching conditions: Restrictions, criteria, priorities
      • Result of advance planning (if performed)
      • Result of target dispatching
      • Predicted entry of the vehicle into the depot (if ITCS interface exists)

      Operating Modes

      • Manual: Authorized user assigns a stabling position to the vehicle via the "Vehicle Dispatching" dialog using the "Manual Dispatch" button. Selection: Vehicle, depot, parking space (by meter or track segment); confirmation generates a transfer order.
      • Semi-Automatic: Authorized user initiates vehicle dispatching in the "Vehicle Dispatching" dialog; this then runs automatically.
      • Fully Automatic: Vehicle dispatching is automatically initiated as soon as:
        • Location tracking registers a vehicle entering the depot,
        • Entry of a vehicle is expected according to the predicted arrival time by ITCS,
        • Vehicle passes a corresponding topology node at the depot.

      Process of Advanced Vehicle Dispatching

      Advanced vehicle dispatching is initiated when automatic or semi-automatic vehicle dispatching is triggered.

      Sequential Processing

      For multiple vehicles, the stabling position is assigned on a vehicle-by-vehicle basis in sequential order. The next vehicle is only dispatched after a stabling position has been assigned to the preceding vehicle.

      Process Steps

      1. Target Dispatching: Determines one or more target usage types based on the state of the vehicle to be dispatched.
      2. Qualicision® (PSI-proprietary software): Calculates a solution set with all alternative stabling position assignments and their ranking. The stabling position assignment with the highest rank is the target stabling position.

      Result

      Assignment of the vehicle to be dispatched to the optimal stabling position with regard to the current operational requirements. The assigned stabling position is reserved for the dispatched vehicle and blocked for other vehicle parking.

      Stabling Options

      Vehicles can be continuously dispatched, provided they are located at the depot under consideration or serve a block in the network that ends at that depot.

      • Direct Stabling: immediate assignment to the target stabling position
      • Intermediate Stabling: for an order (workshop, supply) or as a shunting vehicle (Wender)
      • Final Stabling: final stabling without further planned services on the current day

      Components

      • Vehicle Dispatching Dialog – automatic suggestion, manual target stabling position assignment.
      • Stabling Position Dispatching (Stellplatz-Disposition) via context menu.
      • User-scheduled vehicle logic – pre-assigned vehicles are preferentially dispatched.
      • Special cases – including unexpectedly pulling-in vehicles, towed coupling (Schleppverbund), workshop vehicles.

      Call

      • Toolbar Dialog  →  Vehicle Dispatching.
      • Menu Edit  →  Vehicle Dispatching.
      • Context menu in the depot view (vehicle)  →  Stabling Position Dispatching.
      • Double-click in Pull-in List (Einrückliste)/Block List (Umlaufliste) when status is In Progress (Wird durchgeführt) opens vehicle dispatching.

      Typical Workflows

      Dispatching after Pull-in

      1. Open the Vehicle Dispatching dialog.
      2. Automatic Dispatching: Check the system suggestion.
      3. Accept the suggestion  →  communicate stabling position and set route.
      4. Reject the suggestion  →  Manual Dispatching and communicate stabling position.

      Unexpectedly Pulling-in Vehicles

      • Start dialog from menu/toolbar, select vehicle number, then dispatch as above.
      • Towed Coupling (Schleppverbund): after consultation with the workshop, manually dispatch to the workshop (couple vehicles or dispatch separately if necessary).

      Pre-assigned User-Scheduled Vehicles

      • User-scheduled vehicles reserved for blocks are dispatched exactly there (no third-party dispatching to this block).
      • If a gap arises, the vehicle is not dispatched, or the dispatching is cancelled with an error message.

      Monitoring and Checking

      PSItraffic tracks and checks, based on projected topology nodes, whether the dispatched vehicle is stabled in the assigned, planned stabling position.

      • During checking, the current travel time of the vehicle is considered, which it needs to reach the planned stabling position.
      • If dispatched vehicle reaches the planned stabling position within a configurable time frame  →  planned stabling position is transferred to the current stabling position.
      • If vehicle is stabled at a different stabling position  →  warning message.

      Stabling Position Displays

      If display control is part of the scope of delivery: the planned stabling position assignment is transmitted to the stabling position display as soon as the dispatched vehicle drives over a topology node projected for this display.

      Cancellation of Stabling Position Assignment

      If the dispatched vehicle does not reach the assigned stabling position within a configurable time frame  →  the stabling position reservation is cancelled. The stabling position is considered again in the next vehicle dispatching. The cancellation prevents possible disruptions in operations.

      Special Cases

      Vehicles with Fault or Workshop Order

      • Vehicles with a workshop reservation or fault that no longer permit a block service (temporally) are shown a stabling position in the workshop stabling area.
      • In case of a workshop reservation on the current operating day: **Priority** over supplies starting simultaneously or later.
      • Vehicles with a workshop reservation are always supplied beforehand so that they are fully operational after the workshop without further supply and can be subject to final stabling.
      • For processing, vehicles are collected by workshop employees from the buffer area (Stauraum) or the stabling positions designated for faulty/workshop-ordered vehicles and driven to the workshop.

      Vehicles in Final Stabling

      • Vehicles that will pull out again during the day or should not be supplied are stabled directly for final stabling.
      • Even in final stabling, supplies can be carried out; personnel go to the stabling position of the prepared vehicle.
      • After processing: either all activities are completed and the vehicle is available for block dispatching again, or open activities require relocation.

      Result Display

      • Depot view: Vehicles are displayed in their assigned stabling positions; vehicle symbols are displayed in the pull-out direction by default.
      • Tabular views: The assignment of the dispatched vehicle to its stabling position is displayed.
      • Stabling Lineup Plan (Aufstellplan): graphical control of the dispatching including consistency checks.
      • Dispatching affects processes near the entrance.

      Notes

      • Error message if faults/reservations/washing orders exclude a block service.
      • Suggestions can be rejected and manual target stabling positions can be assigned.
      • The BMS calculates a stabling position based on the dispatching rules and the vehicle state.
      • Vehicles can be continuously dispatched, provided they are located at the depot under consideration or serve a block ending there.
