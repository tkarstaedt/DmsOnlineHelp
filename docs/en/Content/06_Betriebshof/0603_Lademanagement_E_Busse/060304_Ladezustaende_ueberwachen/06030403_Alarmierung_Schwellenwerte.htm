Configure Alarming & Thresholds

To detect critical states of charge early, you can define thresholds in PEAK.DMS. When these are reached or undershot, the system generates alarms or notices that are displayed in the monitoring views or output as messages.

Define thresholds

  1. Open the administration or system settings and select the Alarming/Thresholds area (project-specific menu item).
  2. Set a limit for each parameter. Examples:
Parameter Example threshold Reaction
Low SOC 30 % Issue warning; suggest charging order with high priority
Remaining range 20 km Alarm; optionally take vehicle out of service
Charging phase overdue 10 min over planned end Notice to dispatch; check the charging point
Battery temperature (optional) 50 °C Alarm; reduce charging power or abort session

Types of alarming

You can define different alarm types and actions:

  • Visual warning: Colored highlighting in monitoring views (e.g., red for critical SOC).
  • Acoustic/pop-up message: In the Control Center, a message with a recommended action appears.
  • Automatic order: The system creates a charging order or transfer order with high priority.
  • Notification by email/SMS: Optional, to responsible parties (e.g., garage manager).

Best practices

  • Set thresholds so that sufficient reaction time remains; thresholds that are too low lead to hasty actions.
  • Test alarming processes regularly to ensure that all notification channels work.
  • Consider seasonal differences (winter/summer), as energy demand can fluctuate — adjust thresholds accordingly.
  • Use the capacities and reserve values stored in the electrical master data as the basis for your calculations (see Electrical master data ).
