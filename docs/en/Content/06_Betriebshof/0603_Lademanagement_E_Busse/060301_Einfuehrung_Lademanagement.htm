      Introduction to Charge Management

      Charge management in PEAK.DMS ensures that electric vehicles are ready for operation at the pull-out time and that energy is used efficiently. It plans when and how e‑buses are charged, integrates with block planning and vehicle telematics, and considers charging power, infrastructure, and operational rules.

      Basic Principles

      A modern charge planning system plans not only the power, but also the timing of the charging process.

      • Automatic planning of charging processes in the depot ensures that vehicles are ready on time and that energy is used at optimal rates.
      • Integration with route planning and telematics uses real-time data such as State of Charge (SOC), position, and battery status to dynamically adjust charging plans.
      • Charge planning prioritizes vehicles by pull-out time or energy deficit and uses off‑peak times to reduce costs.

      Energy Amount and Charging Time

      The amount of energy to be charged depends on the battery capacity and the current SOC. Charge management considers battery curves, maximum charging power, and limited grid capacity.

      • Calculation of the required energy amount based on SOC and planned deployment.
      • Consideration of charging power (kW), charging duration, and possible charging curves (e.g., fast charging vs. standard).
      • Optimization of charge planning within the available grid power to avoid load peaks.

      SOC‑Forecasts and Balancing

      SOC‑forecasts estimate the state of charge at a future time based on the operating profile, climate, and route length. Balancing ensures that available energy is distributed fairly among vehicles.

      • Forecast models consider the timetable, route, and energy consumption (incl. air conditioning).
      • Balancing‑algorithms distribute the available grid or battery buffer potential across multiple simultaneous charging processes.
      • Reserve capacity ensures that unforeseen trips (special trips, shunting trips) are covered.

      Support for All Drive Types

      PEAK.DMS supports not only battery buses but also vehicles with diesel, hybrid, and fuel cell drives. Corresponding supply and refueling types are used for these drive types; charging processes apply specifically to battery-electric vehicles.
