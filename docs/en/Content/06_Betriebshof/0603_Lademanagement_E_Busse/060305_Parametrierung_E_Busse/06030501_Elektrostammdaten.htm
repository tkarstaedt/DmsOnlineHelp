      Edit electrical master data

      The electrical master data is part of the vehicle dossier and is displayed only for vehicles with primary propulsion type "Electric." In this dialog you maintain basic information on the traction battery, preconditioning, and energy demand for heating and air conditioning. Correct maintenance of this data is essential for charging planning and range forecasting.

      Battery

      The Battery section contains technical parameters of the traction battery:

      Field Description
      Cumulative total capacity Total capacity of the battery in kWh (manufacturer specification).
      Usable total capacity Capacity usable for the DMS in kWh (excluding reserve).
      Reserve capacity Energy in kWh that is not used for dispatching (safety reserve).
      Balancing method Method for balancing the cells (active/passive).
      Balancing duration Duration of the balancing process in minutes.
      Max. propulsion demand winter Energy demand per kilometer in winter in kWh/km.
      Max. propulsion demand summer Energy demand per kilometer in summer in kWh/km.
      K value 150 Parameter for charge time forecasting (kW).
      Maximum range Range guaranteed by the manufacturer in km.
      Installation date Date on which the battery was installed.

      Preconditioning

      In the Preconditioning section, define the times and power required to prepare the vehicle before departure:

      Field Description
      System Time to achieve operational readiness (24V, compressed air, traction battery preheating) in minutes.
      Climatic (windows ice-free) Time until the windows are ice-free (min).
      Climatic preconditioning Time until the passenger compartment reaches the target temperature (min).
      Duration Total duration of preconditioning.
      Max. power Maximum electrical power for preconditioning.
      Type Type of energy supply (fuel-based, electric).

      Heating/AC

      The Heating/AC section contains parameters for climate energy demand:

      Field Description
      Max. heating duration w/o preconditioning Time until interior temperature is reached without preconditioning (min).
      Max. cooling duration w/o preconditioning Time until interior temperature is reached without preconditioning (min).
      Max. climate energy demand Maximum energy demand of the air conditioning on route (kWh).
      Max. heating energy demand Maximum energy demand of the electric heating on route (kWh).
      Switch-on temperature electric Temperature at which the electric heating switches on (°C).
      Switch-on temperature fuel-based Temperature at which the fuel-based heating switches on (°C).

      Energy-saving mode

      The energy-saving mode can reduce energy consumption when SOC is low:

      • Manual turtle mode: Enable this option to control turtle mode manually.
      • Automatic turtle mode %: Set the SOC threshold at which the mode is activated automatically.

      Best practices

      • Adjust usable capacity as batteries age to create realistic charging plans.
      • Use the balancing parameter to extend battery lifetime. Longer balancing phases can affect vehicle availability but should be accounted for in charging planning.
      • The reserve capacity should be high enough to allow transfer moves or short-notice deployments without additional charging.
      • Settings for heating and air conditioning directly affect energy demand and should be adjusted by season.
