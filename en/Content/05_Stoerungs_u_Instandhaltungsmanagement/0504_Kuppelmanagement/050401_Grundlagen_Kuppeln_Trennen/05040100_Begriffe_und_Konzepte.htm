      Terms & Concepts: Coupling Orders

      This topic defines central terms of coupling management. are used as a generic term and include Decoupling Orders. The same dialog is used for editing both types; it only adjusts the title.

      Coupling Order (Generic Term)

      A controls the state of a Vehicle Pair at a Coupling Point. It has a Type (Couple or Decouple) and passes through a Status Model (e.g., Planned → Confirmed/Canceled → Completed).

      Typical Attributes

      • Order Type: Couple | Decouple
      • Vehicle Pair: A/B (Numbers), Compatibility (Coupling System, Class, Length, Energy)
      • Coupling Point: Location (/Stop/Track), Time (Planned), optional Buffer
      • Reference: Line//Duty
      • Source: (manual/automatic), Import, Rule
      • Status: Planned, Active, Confirmed, Canceled, Completed
      • Meta: Priority, Comment/Reason, Creator/Confirmer (Roles)

      Vehicle Pair

      Two vehicles that can be joined or separated as a Traction. Relevant properties: Couplability, Energy/SoC, Vehicle Class, Length, , Block Status.

      Coupling Point

      Location‑Time reference for the action (e.g., /Track, Stop). A should provide sufficient buffer and the necessary infrastructure.

      Status Model & Lifecycle

      1. Created (from specification/rule/import; optionally manual)
      2. Active (dispatchable in the )
      3. Confirmed → immediate system effect (Coupling/Decoupling is operationally executed)
      4. Canceled → Restriction Flags on both vehicles (see below)
      5. Completed (Archive/Proof)

      Restriction Flags (Fault Markers)

      Upon cancellation, the system creates non-service-preventing faults on both vehicles. These contain the flag “Do not couple” or “Do not decouple”, depending on the order type. The flags serve as a binding instruction to and the Automatic Vehicle Dispatchingnot to suggest the corresponding action again in the future.

      Roles & Responsibilities

      • Dispatching: Operate the , confirm/cancel, comment.
      • Technical Admin: Maintain rules/compatibility, process escalations.
      • /Supply: Provide blocking/safety statuses that affect couplability.

      Contexts

      • Coupling Orders (List, Toolbar, , Dialog)
      • Dialog: Coupling Order (one dialog, two modes)
      • Calendar: Blocks (Events and effect on the timeline)
      • Manual Dispatching (Conflict Resolution)
      • Automatic Vehicle Dispatching (Respects Flags)
