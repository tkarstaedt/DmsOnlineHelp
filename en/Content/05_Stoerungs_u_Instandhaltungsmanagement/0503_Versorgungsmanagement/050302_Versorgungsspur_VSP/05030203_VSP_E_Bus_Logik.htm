      Supply track – e-bus logic

      For electric vehicles, the supply track considers SOC, charging windows, balancing, and preconditioning. The goal is operationally stable, energy-optimized pull-out.

      Dispatching targets

      These key figures determine queuing and duration of charging steps.

      • Target SOC: Minimum SOC for the next block (including reserve).
      • Remaining range: Forecast with/without block reference.
      • Balancing need: Required balancing duration before full charge.
      • Preconditioning: Need and duration for systems/climate.

      Parameters & effect

      Parameter Effect on VSP
      Current SOC [%/kWh] Determines start priority of charging.
      Target SOC [%] Stop criterion for charging; release for pull-out.
      K-value / charging characteristics Charging duration forecast; influences order.
      Balancing (method/duration) Additional step after charging; may be required.
      Heating/AC needs Additional energy before pull-out; may raise target SOC.
      Energy-saving mode Limits power draw; can extend wait time.

      Typical sequence (e-bus)

      The exact sequence depends on stations, parameters, and priorities.

      • Connect to charger > enable charging > charge to target SOC.
      • Balancing (if required).
      • Preconditioning (systems/climate) before pull-out.

      Conflict resolution & fallbacks

      In bottlenecks, the VSP provides controllable alternatives.

      • Re-order to a free charger of the same class.
      • Intermediate target (e.g., interim SOC) with later top-up.
      • Manual override by authorized users.

      Data sources

      E-parameters originate from the vehicle dossier and process data streams.

      • Master data: capacities, K-value, thresholds.
      • Process data: current SOC, charging phase, remaining range, timing information.

      SSB rules for e-bus VSP

      • Queue policy (arrival vs. priority control; reserve slots)
      • Charging strategy (opportunistic vs. window-based; minimum SOC before pull-out)
      • Conflict resolution (parallel assignment, rerouting to alternatives)
      • Preconditioning (if configured; time/SOC-triggered)

      Status & events

      VSP shows status changes (assigned, in servicing, completed) and technical events (error/abort). Relevant changes flow into dispatching and the depot view.
