      System Overview

      The PEAK.DMS system consists of several central components that are closely connected and together form the operational data foundation.

      System Components

      • Server system: The core of the system is the operational data model, which technically represents the operational database. This model continuously stores the current operational situation and makes it available to the internal software modules. Each module fulfills a specific function within PEAK.DMS. Information is exchanged between modules via the shared operational data model. The number of modules in use determines the functional scope of the system.
      • Client: The client is the workplace application installed on users' computers. It accesses data from the operational data model and provides a two‑dimensional graphical user interface for daily work.

      Modularity (SSB)

      The system is modular. At SSB, areas such as Dispatch, Depot View, Workshop/Supply, and Reporting are configured project‑specifically. Modules can be activated independently; interfaces connect timetabling/block and control center systems.

      Modules in use (examples)

      • Control Center/Dispatch (manual, semi‑automatic, and fully automatic)
      • Workshop & Supply (incidents, orders, charging/refueling)
      • Depot View & Stabling (track/stabling views, parking position logic)
      • Quality Management & Reporting (KPIs, exports)

      Data Foundation

      The data of the central operational data model essentially includes:

      • the current planned timetable, including disposition changes affecting individual vehicles, trains, or vehicle groups
      • the current timetable status of a vehicle or train as well as predictions for the further course of the trip
      • the current location information based on transmitted process data, as well as the vehicle or train status
      • the status of monitored devices or infrastructure components (e.g., tracks)

      Interfaces

      PEAK.DMS integrates a wide range of interfaces to existing external systems. Technically, a distinction is made between offline and online interfaces:

      • Offline interfaces: File‑based exchange where provision and import are decoupled in time.
      • Online interfaces: Direct real‑time data transfer between systems.

      Interfaces to data‑providing systems

      • Timetabling: Import of the planned timetable from a third‑party system via a standardized interface.
      • Duty and block scheduling: Ingestion of duty segments with driver and block assignments from third‑party systems.
      • Master data: Transfer of infrastructure elements or vehicle master data from external sources.
      • Refueling data: Transfer of refueling information from third‑party systems at configurable intervals.

      Process connectivity (real‑time interfaces)

      • On‑route positioning information: Periodic transmission of current location data for a vehicle or train via external positioning systems.
      • Depot positioning information: Transmission of parking positions of vehicles based on radio signals or other localization technologies.

      Interfaces to data‑consuming systems

      Data provided by PEAK.DMS can be processed in downstream systems, for example:

      • Passenger information systems: Delivery of real‑time operational information (especially arrival and departure times).
      • Displays at depots, stops, and stations: Provision of vehicle‑ and stop‑related information for drivers and passengers.
