      Timetable Data and DMS‑Calendar

      This chapter describes how timetable data from planning is adopted into PEAK.DMS and the role the DMS‑calendar plays in dispatching.

      Overview

      Timetable data forms the foundation for nearly all operational planning and control processes in the DMS. They are typically imported via the VDV‑452 interface (e.g., from DIVA) and stored as a base version. After content review and approval, they are transferred to the operational data foundation.

      Data Flow Overview

      1. Planning System: Generates export (e.g., VDV-452).
      2. Import into DMS: Creation of a base version.
      3. Review & Approval: Dispatcher/Technical admin checks data.
      4. Transfer to Operations: Approved version becomes effective.
      5. DMS‑Calendar: Assigns operating days to day types and acts as a control point for duty lineup and dispatching.

      Central Terms

      • Operating Day: A calendar day, possibly shifted by the operational day change.
      • Day Type: Classification (e.g., Weekday, Saturday, Holiday, Vacation).
      • VDV‑Calendar: Assignment Operating Day ↔ Day Type, usually imported.
      • DMS‑Calendar: Operational calendar basis with all operating days and day types.
      • Base Version: Imported timetable data delivery before approval.

      Relationships

      The DMS‑calendar is the central hub between imported timetable data and operational applications:

      • Duty lineup planning (see "Duty Lineup Plan" Dialog ) uses day types to correctly form blocks and duties.
      • Vehicle dispatching (see Vehicle Dispatching – Introduction) is based on the active operating days.
      • Area calendar (see Calendar: Blocks) reflect the operational implementation for each functional area.
