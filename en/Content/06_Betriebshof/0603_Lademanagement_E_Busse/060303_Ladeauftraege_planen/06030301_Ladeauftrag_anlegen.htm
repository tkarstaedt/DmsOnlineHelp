      Create Charging Order

      With a charging order, you determine when an electric vehicle should be charged at a charging station and how much energy it should receive. The following steps show how to create a new charging order and define the required parameters.

      Steps to create an order

      1. Open the Charging Orders view in the depot or dispatching interface. Select New… or Create Charging Order… (depending on the project setup).
      2. In the Vehicle field, select the vehicle to be charged from the list. Only vehicles with the primary drive type "Electric" can be selected.
      3. In the Start time field, enter the date and time when the charging process should begin. Consider pull-out times and the expected remaining range of the vehicle.
      4. Optional: In the End time/Deadline field, enter the time by which the vehicle must have reached the desired charge level. This end time can be determined based on the block planning.
      5. In the Energy demand or Target SOC field, enter either the required amount of energy in kWh or the desired state of the battery (SOC) (e.g., 80%). The necessary energy amount depends on the remaining range and the upcoming deployments.
      6. In the Charging point field, select an available charging point. By default, only charging points that are free at the start time are displayed. The charging power can vary per charging point; if necessary, adjust the maximum power (see Define Charging Power).
      7. Assign a Priority (high, normal, low) if necessary. If the charging infrastructure is heavily utilized, orders with higher priority are processed preferentially.
      8. In the Comment/Note field, enter additional information, e.g., special requirements, planned blocks, or restrictions.
      9. Confirm the order with OK or Save. The charging order appears in the list and is considered by dispatching.

      Field Description

      The fields in the Create Charging Order dialog may vary depending on the project. In the default configuration, the following fields are available:

      Field Description
      Vehicle The electric vehicle to be charged (Bus No./Type).
      Start time Start of the charging process.
      End time/Deadline Optional time when charging should be completed.
      Energy amount / Target SOC Required energy amount (kWh) or target SOC (%).
      Charging point Selected charging point with a specific maximum power.
      Priority Urgency level (high/normal/low).
      Comment Free text field for special notes.

      Notes and Best Practices

      • Plan charging orders as early as possible in coordination with the timetables so that there is enough time for the charging process.
      • Utility contracts or local electricity tariffs may provide for favorable charging times outside of peak loads【736875587044737†L190-L199】. Consider these for cost-effective charging windows.
      • Note that the charging speed decreases during the charging process, especially from about 80% SOC; therefore, ensure that the target SOC is sufficient and is reached by the pull-out time.
      • For buses with hybrid drives, charging orders can be created optionally. In the parameters of the vehicle dossier (Electrical Master Data), you can define whether the vehicle is charged at the charging station (see Edit electrical master data).
