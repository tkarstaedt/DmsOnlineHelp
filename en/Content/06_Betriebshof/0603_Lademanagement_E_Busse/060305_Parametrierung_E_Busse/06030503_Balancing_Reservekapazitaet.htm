      Configure balancing and reserve capacity

      Balancing ensures that all cells of a battery are charged evenly, which increases service life and optimizes capacity utilization. Reserve capacity ensures that an energy buffer is always available for unforeseen trips. This topic describes how to maintain both parameters in the DMS and what impact they have.

      Balancing

      During balancing, the cells of the battery are equalized. Depending on the vehicle manufacturer and battery type, active or passive methods can be used. The DMS stores the balancing method and the balancing duration (minutes) in the master data. These values are used for charge completion forecasts and influence the planning of charging orders.

      Settings

      • Balancing method: Select from the available methods (active, passive). Active means that the battery transfers energy to other cells during balancing; passive means that excess energy is converted into heat.
      • Balancing duration: Define how long the balancing process usually takes. Longer balancing phases can limit vehicle availability.
      • Balancing time window: Optionally define times (e.g., at night) when balancing is preferred to avoid affecting charging and block plans.

      Reserve capacity

      Reserve capacity is the amount of energy that is not scheduled. It serves as a buffer for unforeseen transfer moves, garage trips, or other emergencies. In the electrical master data, define the level of reserve capacity in kWh.

      Recommendations

      • Set reserve capacity proportional to total capacity (e.g., 10â€“20% of usable capacity). Too little reserve can lead to unplanned stops, while too much reserve limits the usable amount of energy.
      • Consider operational specifics such as long transfer routes or lack of charging points in outlying areas.
      • Adjust reserve capacity seasonally: In winter, a larger buffer may be advisable because energy demand (heating, preconditioning) increases.

      Impact on dispatching

      • Balancing is taken into account by the system when calculating charge completion. Charging orders should be scheduled so that balancing phases are completed before the vehicle is used again.
      • Reserve capacity reduces the energy available for blocks. Plan trips so that the reserve is not undershot. This is automatically considered in the range forecast.
      • During maintenance or battery replacement, balancing and reserve parameters must be reviewed and adjusted if necessary.
