      Introduction

      This user manual is the fundamental documentation for new and experienced users of the PEAK.DMS at SSB. It provides the knowledge required to operate the system safely and to implement operational processes effectively.

      The manual also serves as:

      • Overview: You will learn how the PEAK.DMS client is structured and which object-specific display and operating options are available.
      • Textbook: You will be enabled to define and apply operational measures in the system.
      • Reference: Step-by-step instructions guide you through essential operations in the user interface.

      Figures may vary slightly from the actual representation in the graphical user interface depending on system configuration. These deviations do not affect the described functionality.

      For better readability, gender-specific forms of address are omitted. The chosen form includes all genders ( m/f/d ) equally.

      Reading Notes

      The following elements are used in this manual to aid orientation and provide structured instructions:

      • Step-by-step instructions:
        • In the text, indicated by numbered lists (1., 2., 3., etc.).
        • In figures, marked by red numbers in circles.
      • Optional actions:
        • In the text, indicated by an asterisk (*) symbol.
        • In figures, marked by gray numbers in circles.
      • References to fields in views and dialogs:
        • In the text, by lettered lists (a., b., c., etc.).
        • In figures, marked by green circles or droplets with letters.
        • Yellow highlights indicate fields to be edited.
      • Figures: Serve as graphical support. Non-relevant information is hidden or obfuscated. Labels in figures are illustrative.
      • Notes: In the print version, space is provided at the bottom of each page for personal notes.
      • Information boxes: Special notices are highlighted with dedicated info icons.
      • User actions: Actions in the user interface are shown in bold.
      • Cross-references: Internal references appear as hyperlinks and lead directly to the referenced chapter, dialog, or view (e.g., <View> or <Dialog>).
